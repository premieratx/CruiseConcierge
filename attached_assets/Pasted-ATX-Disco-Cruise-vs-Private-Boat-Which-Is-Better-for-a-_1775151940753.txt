Private Party Boat in Austin: What You Need to Know Before You Book One
Meta Description: Renting a private party boat on Lake Travis? Here’s what to know before you book. From boat sizes and costs to BYOB rules, captain info, and planning tips, we cover all the essentials so your Austin private boat party goes off without a hitch.

Choose the Right Boat Size and Type
Party boats aren’t one-size-fits-all. In Austin, you’ll find options from small pontoons to huge double-decker barges:
Smaller Pontoons (up to ~14 people): Great for intimate groups. Cheaper hourly rates, easier to maneuver. Downside: less space to dance or move around, fewer amenities.


Mid-Size Party Boats (15-30 people): These are popular for bachelor/bachelorette groups. Often have a shaded deck, some seating, maybe a basic sound system. A good balance of space vs. cost.


Large Double-Deckers (31-75 people): Essentially floating event spaces. Lots of room, likely a second level, possibly a slide, more amenities. Higher cost (and sometimes limited availability), but if you have a huge group or want to go big, this is it.


Tip: Don’t try to cram more people than allowed. Boat capacity isn’t just about comfort, it’s a legal safety limit. If you’re on the fence with group size, err on the side of a slightly bigger boat so everyone has room.
Pricing 101: Hourly Rates, Fees, and Timing
Private boats typically rent by the hour with a minimum number of hours (often 4 hours). Key cost factors:
Day of Week & Time: Saturdays are peak = highest prices. Weekdays or early morning slots can be cheaper. Sunset slots might have a premium because, well, sunsets are awesome.


Boat Size: Bigger boat, bigger price tag (not just for the boat, but often additional crew fees if the group is large).


What’s Included: Check if the rate includes the captain and fuel. With Premier, crew fees are included for most groups (only extra if 26+ people), and fuel is usually included for standard cruising. Some outfits might add a fuel surcharge or captain fee – clarify this!


Also, plan timing carefully. A “4-hour rental” includes time to get everyone on and off the boat. If you show up late, you usually don’t get extra time (boat schedules are tight on busy days). So tell your friends to be punctual – bachelor time can’t be a thing if you want max lake time.
And remember sales tax (~8.25%) and gratuity for the crew (15-20%) when budgeting.
BYOB and Food – The Rules
Most Austin party boats, including ours, are BYOB – bring your own beverages (21+ for alcohol, of course). A few guidelines:
No Glass: Almost all companies ban glass beer bottles or drinkware for safety. Opt for cans or plastic. Champagne bottles are usually allowed for toasts (just be careful with glass).


Coolers & Ice: Check if they’re provided. We include coolers with ice on our boats, so you can arrive with drinks already cold. If not provided, you’ll need to bring your own with ice.


Food: BYO snacks or catering is usually fine. Finger foods that handle heat (sandwiches, chips, fruit) work best. Some boats (like our big one) even have a grill – ask if you can use it or if crew will grill for you.


Trash & Cleanup: The crew generally handles trash at the end, but keep it tidy as you go so you’re not partying in a pile of empties. Bring trash bags just in case, or confirm they’ll be on board.


Pro tip: Consider using a delivery service for your drinks and ice. We work with Party On Delivery to have alcohol and supplies delivered dockside for private charters. That way you’re not hauling 100 lbs of ice and beer from your car.
Captain and Crew – Know the Deal
You’ll have a licensed captain provided (or a list of approved captains to choose from). Do not attempt to captain yourself unless you’re renting a small boat that explicitly allows it (rare for party barges, plus who wants that responsibility on a party day?). Our captains are Coast Guard certified and super experienced – they handle parking a 50-foot barge like it’s a Honda Civic.
A few tips:
Listen to the Safety Talk: Yes, you’re eager to crack a beer, but pay attention when the captain explains life jackets, emergency procedures, etc. It’s quick and it’s for everyone’s safety.


Crew as Helpers: On many rentals, you’ll have a deckhand too. They’re there to help – whether it’s tying up floats, refilling ice, or snapping a group photo. Treat them with respect (and offer them a burger if you’re grilling – they appreciate it!). They’ve seen it all and often love to joke around and ensure you’re having fun.


Follow Their Guidance: If the captain says “please don’t all crowd to one side” or “storm’s coming, we need to head in,” respect that. They know the lake and boat’s limits.


One more important thing: Don’t Drink and Drive – even boats. On a captained charter, you’re not driving, so relax. Austin’s drag scene is vibrant, sassy, and a ridiculously good time. Treat the bride to a drag brunch – a late morning feast of mimosas, pancakes, and performances by fierce drag queens. If your squad loves the great outdoors and their creature comforts, consider a glamping night in Texas Hill Country. Just outside Austin, you can rent luxury tents or Airstream trailers (check out places like Lake Travis Glamping or Green Acres in Elgin). Spend an evening under the stars with a private campfire, s’mores, and maybe a hired guitarist to strum your bride’s favorite tunes. By day, take a hike or do some yoga by scenic cliffs; by night, crack open the champagne around the fire. It’s a serene escape from the city’s hustle that still feels special – and yes, these sites have real beds and chic decor, so no one has to truly “rough it.” This unique idea gives you a chance to bond with your girls in nature while still Instagramming those boho-chic vibes.

Austin has cute plant boutiques (for example, Succulent Native) that offer private classes where your group can sip wine and craft adorable succulent gardens or flower crowns. It’s therapeutic, a little Zen, and you’ll all have a lovely handmade souvenir to take home. Even friends who swear they aren’t artistic will surprise themselves. And if plants aren’t your thing, consider other crafty classes: candle making, jewelry stamping, or a group painting session (with wine, of course). It’s a fun change of pace that lets everyone flex their creative muscles and make something memorable from the weekend.

Kick off the weekend with some adrenaline! Take the guys to COTA Karting at the Circuit of The Americas – yep, the same complex where the F1 races happen. This is not your average go-kart track. These super fast go-karts let you and your buddies channel your inner race car drivers on a legit track with hairpin turns. Suit up, get your helmets on, and see who can set the fastest lap time. A little friendly competition goes a long way, and the banter over who almost beat whom will fuel the rest of your night. Loser buys the first round of beers later? Deal. It’s a unique rush that beats any video game, and it’s something the groom will never forget (especially if he wins!).
2. Lake Travis Party Barge or Boat Day
If the bachelorette parties get disco cruises, why shouldn’t the bachelors get in on the lake action too? Renting a party barge on Lake Travis is an absolute must for an Austin bachelor weekend. Imagine a huge double-decker boat equipped with a sound system, coolers of beer, and a captain who knows where all the best swim spots are. You can grill on board, jam to your favorite playlists, and leap off the top deck into the lake. For extra laughs, bring some outrageous floaties (giant T-Rex, anyone?) and maybe a football to toss around in the water. It’s like having your own floating backyard barbecue. The vibe can be as wild or relaxed as you want – blast some country tunes and chill with a fishing line in the water, or turn it into a full-on rager. Either way, a sunny day on Lake Travis with the boys, cold drink in hand, is peak Austin. Just don’t forget the sunscreen, and perhaps challenge another boat of party-goers to a dance-off from across the water.
3. Brewery Crawl on a Party Bike
Austin’s craft beer scene is off the charts, so why not explore it in a uniquely Austin way – on a pedal-powered party bike. Gather the crew, climb onto a big pedal trolley, and cruise between some of Austin’s best breweries or beer gardens. The vehicle is BYOB (some allow you to bring a cooler of beer onboard), and a driver steers while you provide pedal power. Hit spots like Zilker Brewing, Lazarus Brewing, or Austin Beerworks – you can customize the route. As you pedal (slowly) through the streets, music blasting, you’ll definitely draw cheers from passersby. It’s a camaraderie builder and a pre-game all in one. Each brewery stop is a chance to taste local IPAs and lagers and maybe get a bite from a food truck. By the end, everyone will be in great spirits and maybe have a new favorite Austin beer. Plus, you’re technically exercising while drinking – talk about multitasking!
4. Urban Axe Throwing Tournament
Unleash your inner lumberjack and book an axe throwing session for the group. Places like Urban Axes or Hatchet Alley will set you up with private lanes, targets, and an instructor to teach you how to safely hurl axes like a Viking. Turn it into a tournament: split into teams, create a bracket, and see who’s the sharpest shooter in the crew. The competitive trash talk and high-fives when someone nails a bullseye make for epic memories (and great video clips). Many axe throwing venues are BYOB or have a bar, so you can sip a beer between throws. It’s a bit unconventional, a bit primal, and a whole lot of fun – perfect for a bachelor party that wants to try something new. Just be prepared: it gets surprisingly addictive trying to hit that perfect throw! The groom might end up with a new hobby.
5. BBQ Tour with a Pitmaster
You can’t come to Austin and not indulge in barbecue – it’s basically a food group here. Instead of just one dinner, make it an entire BBQ tour. Hire a local guide or designate a driver and hop between iconic BBQ spots like Franklin Barbecue, La Barbecue, and Terry Black’s. Yes, it’s a lot of meat, but this is the time to go big or go home. Pro tip: pre-order or go at off-peak times to avoid the longest lines (Franklin, we’re looking at you). At each stop, grab a few pounds of brisket, ribs, and sausage, and have a tailgate-style feast. The groom who loves BBQ will think he’s died and gone to smoked meat heaven. If you want to add a unique twist, see if you can arrange a backstage pit tour – some places will show you their smokers and share stories if you ask nicely (or tip well). By the end of the crawl, you’ll have a definitive answer to the group’s favorite BBQ joint, and probably the itis. Plan for a relaxed evening after this one – meat sweats are real.
6. Sports Day at Topgolf or Chicken N Pickle
Sometimes the best bonding comes with a side of sports. Austin’s got you covered with some unique sporting venues. Topgolf is a classic choice – it’s like a driving range meets lounge. You don’t have to be a golfer to enjoy swinging at targets and eating nachos in a private bay. It’s competitive enough to keep the guys entertained and casual enough that everyone can chat and hang out. If golf isn’t your thing, check out Chicken N Pickle – a cool new spot that combines pickleball (the paddle sport craze) with a casual bar/restaurant scene. Rent a few pickleball courts, and have a tournament while enjoying some beers and chicken wings between matches. It’s half social, half sporty. The mix of friendly competition and chill downtime is perfect for a bachelor group with mixed interests. Winner gets bragging rights, loser might have to wear a silly outfit later that night – your call!
7. Escape Room Mission
For the puzzle-loving groom or if you need an indoor activity, an escape room can be a total blast. Austin has some top-tier escape rooms with elaborate themes – think jewel heists, zombie apocalypses, prison breaks. Book a private session for your group and see if you can all work together to solve the challenges and break out in time. The ticking clock amps up the excitement, and you’ll quickly find out which friend is the code-breaker, who’s the clue-finder, and who’s just there for moral support. Win or lose, it’s 60 minutes of intense fun and teamwork. Many escape room complexes are near downtown, so you can schedule it as a pre-dinner activity. To raise the stakes, make a bet: if you escape, the groom chooses where you all go next; if you fail, someone else picks (or the groom has to do a funny dare later!). Either way, it’s a memorable group challenge that’s way more interactive than sitting around a bar.
8. Comedy Club Roast & Brew
Give the groom a night of laughs he won’t see coming. Start with dinner at a local brewery – Austin has tons with great food, like Austin Beer Garden Brewing (ABGB) or Pinthouse Pizza for pizza and pints. Then head to a stand-up comedy show at a venue like Cap City Comedy or The Creek and The Cave. Let the staff know it’s a bachelor party and you just might get the groom gently roasted by the comedian on stage (if he’s up for it). Some comedy clubs even have open mic portions – dare the boldest friend to get up and tell a joke or two incorporating the groom’s most embarrassing moment. It’s a unique way to infuse humor into the celebration beyond the usual bar jokes. After a few hours of belly laughs, hit West 6th or Rainey Street to cap the night. The inside jokes from the comedy show will keep everyone chuckling for the rest of the trip.
Austin’s slogan might be “Keep Austin Weird,” and embracing that spirit will take any bachelor party from ordinary to extraordinary. The ideas above ensure you get a taste of what makes this city special – speed, sun, suds, steaks, sports, and side-splitting laughter – all tailored into an epic weekend. Mix and match a few of these unique activities with the classic staples (live music and a night out on 6th Street, of course), and you’ll have an itinerary that pleases the whole crew. Most importantly, the groom will get an unforgettable send-off into married life, Austin-style. So gear up, gentlemen – adventure awaits in ATX! 🍺🤘

ATX Disco Cruise vs. Private 14-Person Cruise: Inclusions and Pricing Comparison
Both the ATX Disco Cruise and a Private Boat Cruise (up to 14 people) offer a 4-hour party on Lake Travis, but they differ in experience, inclusions, and pricing. Below is a detailed look at what you get for your money with each option, including all inclusions (vs. add-ons), and a comparison of Friday/Saturday pricing for each experience.
ATX Disco Cruise (Shared Party Experience)
The ATX Disco Cruise is a shared 4-hour party boat experience (March–October) where multiple groups join together on one big party barge. It’s essentially an all-inclusive bachelor/bachelorette party cruise with a fun disco theme. Here’s what you get with an ATX Disco Cruise ticket:
Professional Entertainment: A live DJ is onboard spinning music for the full 4 hours, keeping the energy high, and a professional photographer is included to capture the fun (digital photos are delivered to you for free). You don’t have to hire your own entertainment – it’s built into the experience.


Party Atmosphere & Extras: The cruise provides a disco dance floor, giant floats (including a legendary 25-foot custom unicorn float, exclusive to this cruise), plus multiple lily-pad water mats for swimming. Fun party supplies are included as well – think bubbles, drink koozies, name tags, and even ice-water stations to keep everyone hydrated. The boat has a clean restroom on board for comfort.


BYOB with Amenities: It’s BYOB, but they’ve got you covered with coolers and ice provided for your drinks on the shared cruise. You can bring your own beverages and snacks, and there’s even an option to coordinate alcohol delivery to the marina if needed. Essentially, you just show up with your drinks – the crew handles the rest (coolers, ice, cups, etc.).


Multi-Group Energy: Because it’s a shared event, you’ll be partying alongside other groups (often 50+ people total on the boat). This creates a high-energy, “big party” vibe that many guests say is hard to replicate on a private boat. The crowd is usually filled with bachelor and bachelorette parties, making for a super social atmosphere. (It’s exclusively marketed to bach groups, so everyone on board is in a celebratory mood.)


Turnkey Convenience: The Disco Cruise is fully planned for you – music, entertainment, activities and backup plans are all in place. Notably, it’s the only party boat with a rain contingency plan: if weather spoils the lake day, they invoke the “Lemonade Disco” – moving the party to a downtown Austin club for the same 4 hours with the same DJ and photographer, so your celebration never gets canceled. This guarantee means zero stress about weather.


Package Options: There are tiered ticket packages (Basic, Disco Queen, Super Sparkle) in some promotions, but generally the price per person varies by time slot (see pricing below). All tiers include the core amenities above; higher tiers can add things like VIP decorations, bride perks, or mimosa setups, but even the base ticket is very comprehensive. In short, your ticket price already includes virtually everything you need for an epic party – you just BYOB and enjoy.


Private Boat Cruise (Up to 14 People)
A Private Cruise for 14 or fewer guests is an exclusive charter of a party boat just for your group. With Premier Party Cruises, the 14-person boat (named “Day Tripper”) comes with a captain and basic amenities for a 4-hour Lake Travis cruise. Here’s what you get for your money with a standard private charter:
Your Own Boat & Crew: You have the entire boat to yourselves, with a licensed captain and professional crew provided to operate the vessel safely. The charter includes all the boating essentials: fuel, safety equipment, and life jackets are covered in the base price. A private cruise is fully exclusive – no strangers, just your invitees on board.


Basic Amenities: The boat features a premium Bluetooth sound system (so you can be the DJ with your own playlist) and large coolers for your drinks. The outing is BYOB as well – you bring your own beverages and food. (Ice is available, though note that on a standard private rental you may need to bring your own ice or add a package for it.) The 14-person boat has comfortable seating and a clean restroom, ensuring a comfortable ride. Lily pad float mats and other floaties are available for use when you stop to swim, so your group can relax in the water. (These may be included or small add-ons depending on the package, but they are generally available to enhance your lake time.)


Customizable Experience: One big benefit of a private charter is flexibility. You can typically choose your own cruise time (any day of the week, subject to availability) and aren’t limited to the Disco Cruise schedule. The itinerary can be customized – for example, you can decide when to stop for swimming, or even specify a route or cove if you have a preference. You also set the music and vibe to whatever suits your group (since there’s no outside DJ unless you hire one). This makes private charters popular for a wider range of occasions – not just bachelor/ette parties, but also birthdays, family gatherings, corporate outings, etc., where you might want a more controlled atmosphere.
