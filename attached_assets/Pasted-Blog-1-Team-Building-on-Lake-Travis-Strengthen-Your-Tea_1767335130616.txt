Austin Boat Party Catering: Food and Beverage Coordination for Perfect Events
Fuel the Fun – Good food and drinks can make your boat party go from great to legendary. But catering on a boat has its quirks – space is tight, the environment is mobile, and you can’t exactly order pizza delivery to the middle of Lake Travis (well, not usually!). This section will guide you on coordinating food and beverages for your boat event, ensuring everything is delicious, easy to serve, and mess-minimal. Plus, how to leverage Party On Delivery and coordinate with Premier Party Cruises to handle the heavy lifting.
Choosing the Right Menu
When thinking of what to eat on board, prioritize:
Finger Foods & Portable Snacks: You want items people can grab and eat with one hand, ideally. Think sliders, chicken tenders, wraps, fruit skewers, chips & dip, veggies & hummus cups, charcuterie bites (cheese cubes, etc.). Party On Delivery can help assemble party platters or deliver items from local delispremierpartycruises.com. These are great because no one needs a fork/knife plate situation which can be awkward on a boat.


Minimize Mess: Saucy BBQ ribs? Probably not the best unless you have lots of napkins and a wash station. Also, crumbly foods (like some chips and crackers) will inevitably fall – but that’s okay, just have a little broom or let the crew quickly rinse the deck later (non-glass, food crumbs won’t harm the lake environment if a bit goes over, but we try to keep tidy).


Temperature Considerations: On a hot day, mayo-heavy salads (potato salad, etc.) might spoil – skip those or keep them super chilled. Instead, maybe a pasta salad with vinaigrette that can sit out. Conversely, if it’s cooler weather, a thermos of hot queso dip might solidify quickly in wind – consider items that can be room temp. Party On Delivery often provides coolers and ice to keep things freshpremierpartycruises.com, and some boats have small galley kitchens or warming trays if arranged.


Dietary Variety: If you have a group, cater to common dietary needs. A vegetarian option (like a tray of veggie wraps or caprese sandwiches) is smart. Gluten-free chips or lettuce wraps for those who need. Fruit is universally friendly and hydrating.


Desserts: Keep it simple – cookies, brownies, or cupcakes (cupcakes are great for birthdays on a boat – no cutting needed). Avoid things that melt severely like fancy frosted cakes in 100°F sun (unless you can keep it in a cooler until serving). Party On Delivery can have a local bakery’s cupcakes or a cookie cake delivered to you before departurepremierpartycruises.com.


Drink Coordination 101
We’ve talked a lot about drinks in prior sections (budget, kits vs bottles, etc.). Key points specifically for catering:
Ample Water & Non-Alcoholic: For every boozy beverage, folks should be sipping water too, especially under the sun. Plan at least 2-3 bottles of water per person for a 4-hour cruise. Party On Delivery can deliver cases of water and even some sports drinks or sodas for varietypartyondelivery.com. Pro tip: put a mix of waters and Gatorades in one cooler labeled “Hydration Station” so it’s easy to find.


Ice, Ice, Baby: It cannot be overstated – have LOTS of ice. For chilling drinks, for people to add to cups, etc. It’s easy to underestimate on a hot day where it melts fast. Party On Delivery usually brings plenty if you specify, and Premier’s boats have coolers to dump it in. A good rule is at least 1-2 pounds of ice per person for a few hours (more if also cooling food).


Cocktail Setups: If you want to play bartender but don’t want to bring your entire bar, plan a signature cocktail or two and get the specific ingredients for those. Everything else can be simpler (beer, seltzer, wine). That limits the different stuff needed. Example: decide on a “Tropical Punch” and stock rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, grenadine. Then also have beer and one white wine. That covers most tastes with minimal bottles. Party On Delivery can pre-mix punch in gallon jugs to make it easypremierpartycruises.com.


Serving Supplies: Don’t forget cups (get more than you think – people misplace or re-up, and extras for water). If doing cocktails, straws (paper or reusable – avoid plastic if possible, they might blow into lake), stirrers, and maybe cocktail napkins. A roll of paper towels is useful too for spills. Garbage bags (again, Party On Delivery can include all these staplespartyondelivery.com).


Designate a “Tend”: If you have more elaborate drinks or just to make sure people eat, consider having one person play host/bartender. Not full time, but someone who keeps an eye and occasionally says “Hey guys, sandwiches are here, eat up!” or mixes a batch of margaritas and pours for everyone at once. This ensures the goodies you brought actually get enjoyed and not forgotten in the cooler until the trip is over.


Working with Premier’s Crew
Premier Party Cruises’ crew members are typically super helpful, but remember, they’re primarily there to navigate and ensure safety. However, they often assist with basic hosting tasks:
They’ll help load your food and drink at the dock – but have things in manageable coolers/containers. Don’t hand them 15 grocery bags of loose items; pre-pack into as few containers as possible.


Ask where to set up food. Many pontoons have a table or counter. The crew can point out the best spot that won’t get wet or be in the way of something.


They might have a bottle opener on hand, but bring one to be safe (or use twist caps/cans mostly).


If grilling is an option (some party barges have grills), see if the crew will man the grill or if you need to. Often they might, but don’t assume. If you bring raw meat, you or someone in your party should be prepared to handle the grilling. Alternatively, easier is to grill ahead of time at home and just keep items warm in foil (the sun kinda does that for you!).


The crew will collect trash periodically. It’s polite to consolidate empty cans, etc., in the provided bin or bag. Also, emphasize to guests: NOTHING goes overboard. Even a tiny cherry tomato – don’t toss it in, it could attract fish maybe, but we keep a principle of no litter/food waste.


End of trip: they will help unload, but it’s nice to tidy up so they aren’t scraping salsa off the deck later. Use that paper towel to wipe up spills, etc. Leaving a clean boat is courteous (and some rentals might have a cleaning deposit, so good to avoid any extra charges by keeping it clean).
