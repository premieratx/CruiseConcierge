Bar Hopping on 6th Street (“Dirty 6th”)

No Austin bachelor party is complete without a night on the notorious 6th Street. This downtown stretch of bars and live music clubs is often compared to Bourbon Street – it’s rowdy, it’s wild, and it’s a rite of passage. Start at the west end and make your way east, or vice versa, popping into any bar that catches your eye (you’ll have plenty of options). For a classic start, grab arcade games and drinks at Kung Fu Saloon, then hit up historic dive bars like Shakespeare’s or Buckshot. By 11 PM, 6th Street is buzzing with bachelor and bachelorette parties, college students, tourists – basically a huge street party. Expect packed bars, live bands or DJs blasting, shots flowing, and lots of memorable people-watching. Pro tip: coordinate a meet-up with any bachelorette groups you met on the disco cruise – rolling deep as a big coed crew on 6th Street is legendary fun. Just remember to pace yourselves: the drinks are cheap and the night is long. (And don’t be that bachelor who passes out by 10 PM – hydrate and rally!) For the ultimate 6th Street experience, consider hiring a local party bus or using a ride service to shuttle your group, so everyone can indulge safely and you roll up to the bars like VIPs.

Rainey Street Vibes and Craft Beers

If your groom is more into craft cocktails and quirky bars than neon-lit clubs, Rainey Street is Austin’s other famous nightlife district – and it’s perfect for bachelor groups. This formerly residential street is lined with renovated bungalow houses turned into bars, each with its own character. Think backyard patios strung with lights, food trucks, and live music wafting through the air. Start at Banger’s Sausage House & Beer Garden for dozens of beers on tap (and ridiculous sausage platters to soak up the alcohol). Then hop between spots like Lustre Pearl (the original Rainey bar, with ping-pong and retro vibes), Container Bar (built from shipping containers, with a dance floor), and UnBARlievable (which has a zany circus theme and a mechanical unicorn to ride – yep, seriously). Rainey Street has a more laid-back, hip vibe than 6th Street, but it can still get pretty rowdy on weekends. It’s ideal for that second night of the trip when you want a lively scene that’s a touch more grown-up and Austin-weird. Pro tip: craft beer lovers should also squeeze in time for a brewery tour. Austin’s craft brewery game is strong – hit Zilker Brewing, Austin Beerworks, or St. Elmo Brewing to sample local IPAs and hang at the beer gardens. Many breweries close by 10 PM, so you can do an afternoon “brews cruise” before the night’s main festivities.

Feast on Texas BBQ (Meat Coma, Anyone?)

You can’t bring the boys to Austin and not indulge in some Texas barbecue. It’s practically a rule. Luckily, some of the country’s best BBQ joints are in town, making for a perfect bachelor party lunch or dinner. If you’re up for an adventure (and an early morning), join the line at Franklin Barbecue – widely considered the holy grail of BBQ. They sell out before noon, but tailgating in line with beers can actually be a fun group activity/bonding experience. Not an early riser? No worries – Terry Black’s BBQ on Barton Springs Road has mouthwatering brisket and beef ribs without the insane wait. Load up on smoked meats and classic sides like mac ‘n’ cheese and slaw, then prepare for the subsequent food coma. Another great option is The Salt Lick, a BYOB barbecue oasis in Driftwood (about 30 minutes outside Austin). They’ll set you up with family-style platters under the oak trees – just bring a cooler of your favorite beers (Shiner Bock, anyone?). Many party crews hit Salt Lick on their way back from a lake day or as a hearty first stop on a night out. Wherever you go, come hungry and leave happy (and maybe a few pounds heavier). Outdoor Adventures to Kick off the Weekend

For an outdoorsy groom or just to balance out all that bar crawling, mix in a daytime adventure that gets the adrenaline pumping. Austin’s surroundings offer plenty of thrills. One popular option is heading to a shooting range like The Range at Austin for some friendly target competition – nothing bonds the guys like a bit of boom-stick action (with safety first, of course). If shooting’s not your thing, consider go-karting at K1 Speed or the legendary Circuit of the Americas Karting track, where you can channel your inner Formula 1 driver. Other ideas: zip-lining over Lake Travis, paintball at a local course, or renting jet skis/boats on Lake Austin for a free-form lake day. During warmer months, a river float is a Texas tradition that bachelor groups love – rent tubes and spend a lazy afternoon floating down the San Marcos or Guadalupe River with beers in hand (just don’t forget the sunscreen). For a more chill outdoor vibe, you can’t beat Barton Springs Pool – an iconic spring-fed swimming hole in the heart of the city that’s perfect for a hangover cure dip. The key is to pick an activity that suits your bachelor’s style. If he’s an adrenaline junkie, ramp it up. If he just wants to relax, a casual swim or a round of frisbee golf at Zilker Park might do. Austin’s great outdoors will add a memorable twist to your weekend – and maybe make you feel a tiny bit better about all the calories from the breweries and BBQ.

Live Music & Entertainment

Austin isn’t called the “Live Music Capital of the World” for nothing. If your crew loves live tunes, build an evening around catching a show. Check who’s playing at Stubb’s BBQ (legendary outdoor venue), ACL Live at the Moody Theater, or classic blues spots like Antone’s. Even if you don’t plan ahead, you’ll stumble upon live bands in many bars on Sixth Street or Rainey Street – from country to rock to 80s cover bands. Another uniquely Austin experience is catching a comedy or magic show at Esther’s Follies on 6th Street – this long-running vaudeville-style comedy revue will have your group cracking up (and possibly getting called on stage for a hilarious skit). For a bit of old-school fun, you could also hit up Cidercade – a giant arcade with 100+ games and a wall of craft ciders on tap (it’s all-you-can-play after a small cover fee, so it’s a great pre-game spot with built-in entertainment). And if your bachelor party happens to coincide with a major event like Austin City Limits Festival or SXSW, well, you’ve basically won the jackpot – lean into it and enjoy the city-wide festivities. Bottom line: make time to enjoy why Austin is famous for live entertainment, not just the partying. A killer concert or a quirky show can be the element that takes your bachelor weekend from great to legendary.

Logistics for a Larger, Co-Ed Group

With great (group) power comes great responsibility – basically, more people means a bit more coordination. Here are some joint-specific tips to keep logistics smooth:

Accommodations: Decide if you’re doing one big house for everyone or separate lodging for guys and girls. One big Airbnb can be super fun (late-night hangouts all together, etc.) and often more cost-effective, but make sure there’s plenty of space (and bathrooms!). Some might appreciate separate spaces to retreat to (and to get ready in peace). Maybe find two houses on the same street, or adjacent hotel rooms. There’s no right or wrong – just talk expectations. If cohabitating, establish some ground rules (like who sleeps where, quiet hours if any, etc.). Pro tip: Some Airbnb hosts in Austin specifically cater to big combined parties – they might offer a larger estate or side-by-side duplex units. Snag one of those if you can.

Transportation: Now you’ve got a mixed group of potentially 10-20 people, consider private transport for key times. A party bus or large van for the lake day (so nobody drives), or a shuttle for the night out so you arrive together. It simplifies things big time. Austin has services like Fetii (group rides) and traditional charters. Yes, Ubers are plentiful, but splitting into many cars each time can get messy (and someone might end up at the wrong bar – oops). If budget allows, booking a bus for a 6-hour night stint to bar-hop can actually save headache and ensure no one wanders off alone. Plus, your party on wheels between stops is next-level.

Reservations & Call-Aheads: Big group = you’ll want reservations for meals or activities whenever possible. Call that restaurant weeks in advance for a group table of 15. Book that brewery tour privately if you can. Premier Party Cruises, obviously book ahead
premierpartycruises.com
. Even some bars in Austin (on West 6th or Rainey) can arrange a reserved area for large parties if you contact them. Use the “we have a combined bachelor/bachelorette” card – venues often love hosting those because it usually means lively, respectful (hopefully) fun and good business.

Matching Swag: We touched on themes in earlier sections; even if you don’t go full costume, consider a unifying element. Maybe custom koozies or cups for everyone (two colors – one for bride’s side, one for groom’s?). Or everyone gets a hat or bandana with your wedding hashtag. For pure function, maybe lanyards or bracelets with a contact info in case someone gets lost or (heaven forbid) an emergency – with a larger group in unfamiliar city, can’t hurt. Matching swag not only bonds, it helps identify your herd in a crowded place.

Communication: Start a big group chat or use an app like GroupMe/WhatsApp and include everyone (both sides) before the trip. That way people can introduce themselves, share excitement, coordinate packing (“hey is everyone bringing cowboy boots?” etc.). During the party, use it to drop pins of locations if you splinter or announce meet times. Having one channel keeps info flowing. Also, perhaps designate one or two point people (maybe Best Man and Maid of Honor) to help you quarterback plans on the ground so you as the couple can enjoy more and stress less.

Budget and Shared Expenses: Be clear upfront how things will be paid. Traditionally, wedding party often chips in to cover the couple’s costs during a bachelor/ette. In a joint scenario, that might mean everyone else pitches to cover you both for certain big expenses (like your portions of the boat or dinner). Alternatively, maybe you two want to cover a particular treat since you combined the party (like you pay for the Airbnb as a thank you). Outline roughly who’s covering what to avoid awkwardness. A shared Google Sheet or using apps like Splitwise can help keep track. The good news is some costs will consolidate compared to two separate parties.

Now, a small note: sometimes mixing groups can potentially mix group dynamics or drama. It’s rare on a joyous weekend, but just in case: keep an eye out that no one’s feeling left out (maybe a friend who doesn’t know others well – engage them), and diffuse any tension early (typically not an issue unless an ex or something weird is in the mix – probably not). So basically, be the gracious hosts of this co-ed shindig, introduce folks around, and set a positive inclusive vibe.
